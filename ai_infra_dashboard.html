04 — Regional carbon attribution
Cost & estimated carbon by deployment region
Grid intensity from Electricity Maps annual averages · PUE 1.2 · 200W/vCPU proxy
Carbon estimation methodology: Energy (kWh) = (cost_usd ÷ $0.085/vCPU-hr) × 0.2 kW × PUE 1.2 · Emissions (kg CO₂) = Energy × grid_intensity (gCO₂/kWh) ÷ 1,000,000
Grid intensity sourced from Electricity Maps. PUE from cloud provider regional disclosures. TDP is a proxy assumption.
These are directional estimates for business decision-making — not audit-grade carbon accounting.
05 — Key finding
Carbon intensity by region
gCO₂/kWh · lower is cleaner
Montreal (ca-central-1) is the carbon outlier
The decision this framework surfaces that nothing else does

Montreal runs at 27 gCO₂/kWh — nearly 19× cleaner than Tokyo at 506g. Columbus (us-east-2), the dominant inference region, sits at 375g.

Shifting workload-eligible inference from Columbus to Montreal is a quantifiable carbon decision — not a vague sustainability goal. This is the trade-off no existing dashboard makes visible.
